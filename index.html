General Information

Japan is an island nation located in the Northern Hemisphere, west of the Pacific Ocean. It shares its maritime borders with the Republic of Korea, China, and Russia. The country has a total land area of about 377,975 square kilometers and is divided into 47 prefectures. Its capital city, Tokyo, is a bustling metropolis with nearly 14 million residents, accounting for around 10% of Japan’s total population.

Japan’s national flag, known as the Nisshoki or Hinomaru, features a red circle representing the sun on a white background. The national anthem, “Kimi ga Yo,” is one of the world’s oldest and shortest anthems. The country’s main language is Japanese, and the primary religions practiced are Shintoism, Buddhism, and Christianity. Japan is also home to the Imperial Family, whose members play a symbolic role in national unity and tradition.

As of 2021, Japan’s population was approximately 125 million. The country’s government operates as a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary system, including the House of Representatives (Shugiin) and the House of Councillors (Sangiin). The national currency is the Japanese Yen, and Japan’s major industries include automobiles, electronics, and technology. With a GDP of about US$4.975 trillion and a GDP per capita of US$34,366 (as of 2020), Japan stands as one of the world’s leading economic powers.
